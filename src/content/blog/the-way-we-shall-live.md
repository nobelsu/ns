> Isn’t it ironically cruel how life works?

Life keeps going. There’s no turning back, not at least for as long as time travel hasn't been invented. We lose loved ones, bring life to new ones, and yet in constance, we are the only ones. Life begins from the moment we come to consciousness. It just exists, popping out of thin air, from nothingness to something (to this, I say, we could debate on for longer). And as quickly as it arrives, it disappears back to void, like it was never there, and we would certainly not notice. 

People age and die. People change through time. So objectively, we are our only source of stability. Then, does this imply that to avoid getting hurt, by the friction by which life moves by, that we should attach ourselves to none but ourselves? That in being codependent, we are to expect pain down the line? That in meaning, follows suffering? That in seeking to go up, we better be none surprised to eventually fall?

Perhaps it is ideal to remain solitary. But to be alone, to lack social bindings, is to lead a meaningless life. For it is the past pains that bring us future joys. It is the presence of relativity that amplifies our experiences, be it for better or for worse. So must we embrace the pain wholeheartedly? Wouldn’t the pain negate our joys?

Say, for instance, that one decides this is not a worth it exchange. That the need for constant peace outweighs all. Then life will not bear fruit. They say that we must appreciate every moment of our lives, for it has been granted as a gift to us. That not all souls get this chance to experience the mortal world. Therefore, shall we appreciate our sufferings too? Are they equally gifts?

Still all paths lead the same way. In the end, there shall be no life, and life would have never seemed to have existed. We had no choice in deciding to journey life, and yet the choice remains forced upon us. 

So tell me, how shall we live life?
